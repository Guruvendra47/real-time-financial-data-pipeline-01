12. base64 encoded your snowfloke login password
Why do this?
Kubernetes Secrets (like db-secret.yaml) don't store passwords in plain text; they require the data to be base64 encoded.
Note: Base64 is not encryption. It’s just a way to hide the text from accidental viewing. Anyone with access to the cluster can easily decode it back.

gitbase command
echo -n "Guru00" | base64
echo -n "Qwertyuiop?0101" | base64

terminal command
# Encode Username
[Convert]::ToBase64String([System.Text.Encoding]::UTF8.GetBytes("Guru00"))
# Encode Password
[Convert]::ToBase64String([System.Text.Encoding]::UTF8.GetBytes("Qwertyuiop?0101"))

and your answer copy that replce yaml username and password with base64
